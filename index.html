Store and retrieve with ease

Most decentralized storage services either need you to jump through hoops (like buying some cryptocurrency) or aren't truly decentralized.

With Web3D, things are both easy-to-use and trustless! Upload any data via our API or our web UI for free. The data will end up on a decentralized set of IPFS and Filecoin storage providers.

Getting started is easy

Step 1

Create an WEB3D account and start uploading your files to IPFS. Your data will be accessible on the IPFS network where it is perpetually and verifiably stored by multiple Filecoin storage providers!

Step 2

Use IPFS links when minting your NFT and metadata. As long as your NFT uses IPFS content identifiers (a hash of the data), no one can dispute what content your NFT is referring to!

Have Any Questions?

Frequently asked questions (FAQ) or Questions and Answers (Q&A), are listed questions and answers, all supposed to be commonly asked in some context

How long will the data be stored on Web3D?
Data will be available in IPFS indefinitely and stored in a long-term redundant Filecoin storage agreement with the global community of miners.
What is the upload or file size limit for Web3D?
Web3D accepts storage requests up to 31GiB per upload! Each upload can contain a single file or a directory of files. (If you're using the HTTP API, you'll need to manually split files over 100MB.) Currently, the rate limit is triggered within a 10-second window if the API uses more than 30 requests using the same API key.
Where can I learn more about NFT best practices?
Visit NFT School for information on NFT best practices, as well as a variety of helpful tutorials and how-to guides for NFT developers.
I'm trying to retrieve my content from IPFS using an HTTP gateway, but I'm getting an HTTP error. Does this mean that my content was not successfully stored on Web3D?
unnecessary! For users who do not run their own IPFS nodes, HTTP gateways are a great way to retrieve content from the IPFS network. However, they do introduce a centralized point of failure for user flows. If a given gateway is down, under heavy load, or facing other issues, users accessing content through that gateway may not be able to access the content. In this case, we recommend trying another gateway or running and using your own IPFS node. Also, if the data is not stored on Web3D, then there can be problems with IPFS nodes because a copy of the data provides that data to the gateway. Using Web3D ensures that stored content is broadcast to the web using best practices!
Who can access the data I store on Web3D?
All data uploaded to Web3D is available to anyone who requests it with the correct CID. Do not use Web3D to store any private or sensitive information in unencrypted form.
Can I delete my data on Web3D?
You can delete data associated with your account and it will no longer appear on the Files page of your account. However, this does not prevent nodes in the IPFS decentralized storage network from retaining copies of data indefinitely. Do not use Web3D for data that may need to be permanently deleted in the future.
Is there a limit to the number of files in a directory?
Other than the 31GiB size limit per upload, the service does not enforce any limit. However, if your directory is large or has a lot of files, you may have trouble uploading due to memory issues (especially uploading to a website via a browser or if the directory size is larger than the device memory) or connectivity issues. If this happens to you, we recommend that you split the directory into smaller directories.
How to use Web3D for free?
Filecoin storage providers submit their hard drive capacity to the Filecoin network and receive substantial block rewards for doing so. This translates into real profits for storage providers, incentivizing them to continue contributing additional hard drive space to the Filecoin network. However, when storage providers store data from Filecoin users, they are 10x more likely to win block rewards! Since Web3D participates in the FilecoinPlus program, all data uploaded through the service is eligible for this 10x reward multiplier. This is such a strong incentive for Filecoin storage providers to store user data that they are often willing to offer free storage and retrieval services for a multiple of this block reward. As such, most storage providers offer free storage and retrieval on Filecoin today, and will continue to do so as long as block rewards continue to be a strong incentive. This should be true for a long time - for example, the block reward is still a strong incentive for Bitcoin miners today. While running a Web3D service incurs some additional infrastructure costs, ProtocolLabs is committed to maintaining this infrastructure indefinitely as part of our mission to grow a decentralized storage ecosystem and preserve human information for future generations.
How does one acquire bitcoins?
While it may be possible to find individuals who wish to sell bitcoins in exchange for a credit card or PayPal payment, most exchanges do not allow funding via these payment methods. This is due to cases where someone buys bitcoins with PayPal, and then reverses their half of the transaction. This is commonly referred to as a chargeback.
Can I make money with Bitcoin?
You should never expect to get rich with Bitcoin or any emerging technology. It is always important to be wary of anything that sounds too good to be true or disobeys basic economic rules.
What happens when bitcoins are lost?
When a user loses his wallet, it has the effect of removing money out of circulation. Lost bitcoins still remain in the block chain just like any other bitcoins. However, lost bitcoins remain dormant forever because there is no way for anybody to find the private key(s) that would allow them to be spent again. Because of the law of supply and demand, when fewer bitcoins are available, the ones that are left will be in higher demand and increase in value to compensate.
Who controls the Bitcoin network?
Nobody owns the Bitcoin network much like no one owns the technology behind email. Bitcoin is controlled by all Bitcoin users around the world. While developers are improving the software, they can't force a change in the Bitcoin protocol because all users are free to choose what software and version they use.

How are bitcoins created?
New bitcoins are generated by a competitive and decentralized process called "mining". This process involves that individuals are rewarded by the network for their services. Bitcoin miners are processing transactions and securing the network using specialized hardware and are collecting new bitcoins in exchange.
Why do bitcoins have value?
Bitcoins have value because they are useful as a form of money. Bitcoin has the characteristics of money (durability, portability, fungibility, scarcity, divisibility, and recognizability) based on the properties of mathematics rather than relying on physical properties (like gold and silver) or trust in central authorities (like fiat currencies). In short, Bitcoin is backed by mathematics.
What determines bitcoin's price?
The price of a bitcoin is determined by supply and demand. When demand for bitcoins increases, the price increases, and when demand falls, the price falls. There is only a limited number of bitcoins in circulation and new bitcoins are created at a predictable and decreasing rate
Can bitcoins become worthless?
Yes. History is littered with currencies that failed and are no longer used, such as the German Mark during the Weimar Republic and, more recently, the Zimbabwean dollar.

Is Bitcoin legal?
To the best of our knowledge, Bitcoin has not been made illegal by legislation in most jurisdictions. However, some jurisdictions (such as Argentina and Russia) severely restrict or ban foreign currencies. Other jurisdictions (such as Thailand) may limit the licensing of certain entities such as Bitcoin exchanges.
Is Bitcoin useful for illegal activities?
Bitcoin is money, and money has always been used both for legal and illegal purposes. Cash, credit cards and current banking systems widely surpass Bitcoin in terms of their use to finance crime. Bitcoin can bring significant innovation in payment systems and the benefits of such innovation are often considered to be far beyond their potential drawbacks.
Can Bitcoin be regulated?
The Bitcoin protocol itself cannot be modified without the cooperation of nearly all its users, who choose what software they use. Attempting to assign special rights to a local authority in the rules of the global Bitcoin network is not a practical possibility.
What about Bitcoin and taxes?
Bitcoin is not a fiat currency with legal tender status in any jurisdiction, but often tax liability accrues regardless of the medium used. There is a wide variety of legislation in many different jurisdictions which could cause income, sales, payroll, capital gains, or some other form of tax liability to arise with Bitcoin.
